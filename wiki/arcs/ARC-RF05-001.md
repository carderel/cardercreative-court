## What This Is

Melissa issued 26 documented legal or compliance threats over 4 years. The threats span contempt filings, sole-custody demands, harassment charges, and a rape/assault allegation (May 2024). Despite the volume and escalation of these threats, **none have been documented as executed** in the dataset — a pattern the analysis calls the "paper-tiger trajectory."

The threats function as a behavioral control mechanism: they create ongoing legal anxiety in Eli without requiring follow-through, suppressing his willingness to assert his parental rights.

---

## The Paper-Tiger Trajectory

A legal threat has a definable lifecycle: issued → escalated → deadline passed → not executed → reset. This arc documents that lifecycle repeating across 26 signals.

**Phase A (2022–2025):**
Early threats established the pattern: contempt filings for minor schedule disputes, sole-custody demands tied to Abby interactions, harassment threat for text messages. Threats escalated in severity over time but none executed.

**Phase B (May–Aug 2025):**
Peak threat density. Eli's AI-guided communication produced measurably de-escalated messages — Melissa's threats continued regardless, confirming the threats were not responses to Eli's behavior but a control mechanism.

**Phase C — Agreement-Ready State (Sep 2025):**
Analysis documents that as of September 2025, Melissa had reached an "agreement-ready state" — the threat trajectory had peaked and Melissa was signaling openness to resolution. This is significant: the legal escalation (police report, court filings) in Phase C may have shifted the threat calculus.
