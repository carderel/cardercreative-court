## What This Is

De-escalation resistance documents a specific finding: Melissa's hostile behavior **does not respond to Eli's de-escalation attempts**. Eli's consistent de-escalation posture, and the measurably more de-escalatory Phase B communication, produced no corresponding reduction in Melissa's hostile behavior.

This arc isolates and names that finding as a behavioral characteristic: Melissa is de-escalation-resistant.

---

## The Phase B Evidence

Phase B is the strongest evidence for this arc. From May 4, 2025 onward, Eli used AI-guided communication that produced objectively calmer, more measured messages. This is a real behavioral change — verifiable in the messages.

Melissa's hostile episode density did not decrease. Her escalation behavior continued. This confirms the finding: her behavior is **not contingent on Eli's behavior**. It is self-generating.

---

## Court Argument Value

De-escalation resistance is relevant to any proposed co-parenting therapy or mediation order: the record shows that Eli's unilateral de-escalation produced no results over 4 years. Any court-ordered process that depends on both parties responding constructively should be evaluated against this documented history.
