**Status:** ACTIVE — open at dataset close

**Date Range:** August 2022 – April 2026
**Dataset Phases:** A, B, C (full span)

---

## What This Is

This arc documents Melissa's sustained campaign to establish in Olivia's mind — and in the co-parenting record — that Eli is an inadequate, absent, and potentially harmful father. The campaign operates through three interlocking mechanisms:

1. **Abandonment framing** — positioning Eli's normal absence (work, separate household) as emotional abandonment that Olivia experiences and reports
2. **Abby attack vector** — characterizing Eli's girlfriend Abby as dangerous, mean, and unwanted, creating structural conflict between Olivia's relationship with Eli and Olivia's coached rejection of Abby (since Abby lives with Eli, rejecting Abby = restricting Eli visits)
3. **Inadequate-father narrative** — explicit and implicit characterizations that Eli doesn't make effort, doesn't love Olivia as much as Milo and Alice, or is incapable of making good choices

The arc was active from the first batch and is intact at dataset close.

---

## How It Developed

**Phase A (Aug 2022 – Apr 2025):**
The narrative was established early (ROW-43: church incident relayed to reframe Eli as aggressive; ROW-361-363: Olivia's grief about abandonment delivered as guilt relay). The Abby attack vector intensified in April 2023 when Melissa began conditioning Olivia's visits on Abby's exclusion — claiming Olivia was "adamant" and that this was "her plan all on her own" (ROW-2408). By mid-2023, Melissa was using Olivia's stated preferences to structurally restrict Eli's access.

**Phase B (May 2025 – Aug 2025):**
The Abby attack vector continued with Gary-comparison framing introduced (Apr 2026): *"Gary's seen her more in the last two months than you have and she's not his kid"* — positioning a new male figure as a better father than Eli.

**Phase C (Sep 2025 – Apr 2026):**
The abandonment framing merged with the visit-refusal cascade: Olivia's coached visit refusals were framed as evidence of Eli's inadequacy rather than the product of Melissa's coaching. The arc remained active at dataset close.

Since Abby lives with Eli, this created structural conflict between Olivia's attachment to her father and her conditioned rejection of his household.

*"Gary's seen her more in the last two months than you have and she's not his kid."* First direct positioning of Gary as a more attentive parental figure than Eli. New mechanism in supplemental period.

## Court Argument Value

This arc establishes a **systematic alienation pattern** — not isolated critical remarks. The Coaching Admission (ROW-6819) is particularly powerful because it gives the court a framework for evaluating all of Olivia's expressed preferences: they were not independent.
