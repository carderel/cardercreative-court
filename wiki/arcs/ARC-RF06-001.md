# ARC-RF06-001 — Financial Leverage Pattern

**Status:** ACTIVE
**RF Category:** [[../patterns/RF-06|RF-06 — Financial Coercion]]
**Signal Count:** 12
**Date Range:** 2022 – April 2026
**Dataset Phases:** A, B, C

---

## What This Is

Melissa uses financial demands, childcare billing, and cost-shifting as leverage against Eli — particularly during periods when she is denying him parenting time. The pattern compounds the access-denial mechanism in RF-02: Eli is denied visits and then billed for childcare costs that arise from his denied access.

Key mechanisms documented:
1. **Billing on refused visits** — Eli charged for childcare when Melissa denies the visit that would have made childcare unnecessary
2. **Cost-shifting transport** — logistics costs shifted to Eli as a condition of accessing his parenting time
3. **Phase C contempt threat with specific financial demand** — a concrete financial compliance demand issued alongside a specific execution date

---

## Key Signals

**Billing on Refused Visits**
The most structurally unfair pattern: Melissa refuses a visit (RF-02), then charges Eli for the resulting childcare expense that only exists because the visit was refused. Creates a financial penalty for asserting parenting rights.

**Phase C — Contempt Threat + Execution Date**
A specific financial compliance demand issued in Phase C with a concrete deadline — the most operationalized financial threat in the arc.

---

## Court Argument Value

The billing-on-refused-visits pattern is particularly useful for a motion to compel: it demonstrates that the access denial has financial consequences for Eli that compound over time, and that Melissa is simultaneously profiting from the denial.

Cross-documents: RF-02 (access denial), RF-03 (transport/logistics), RF-05 (contempt threats).
