# ARC-RF07-001 — Communication Boundary Violation Pattern

**Status:** ACTIVE
**RF Category:** [[../patterns/RF-07|RF-07 — Boundary Violations]]
**Signal Count:** 12
**Date Range:** 2022 – April 2026
**Dataset Phases:** A, B, C

---

## What This Is

Melissa repeatedly violates communication boundaries — sending high volumes of messages in compressed timeframes (flooding), demanding responses, continuing contact after explicit requests to stop, and intruding on Eli's parenting time with non-urgent messages.

**Documented mechanisms:**
1. **Flooding** — confirmed episode Apr 2026; Aug 2024 and Aug 2025 mega-clusters
2. **Response-demand pressure** — messages framed as requiring immediate response
3. **Continuation-after-stop** — contacting Eli after he has explicitly asked for a break
4. **Custodial-time intrusion** — contacting during Eli's parenting time for non-emergencies

---

## Key Signals

**Aug 2024 Mega-Cluster**
A high-volume message cluster that constitutes one of the most significant flooding events in the dataset. Sev-2+.

**Aug 2025 Mega-Cluster**
Second major flooding event, Phase B. Eli's AI-guided communication was in effect — Melissa's flooding behavior continued regardless.

**Apr 2026 — Confirmed Flooding**
Final flooding confirmation in supplemental period. Flooding behavior persisted into Phase C.

**"Stop texting me" then continues**
Documented instance where Eli explicitly asked to stop and Melissa continued. This is the most legally clear-cut boundary violation.

---

## Court Argument Value

Communication flooding is documentable through message timestamp analysis. The continued contact after explicit requests to stop may support harassment arguments. The persistence of flooding into Phase C is notable — it suggests the behavior is compulsive rather than tactical.

Cross-documents: RF-05 (harassment threats), RF-04 (DARVO — Melissa accuses Eli of harassment while exhibiting flooding).
