## Important Note on Signal Count

RF-10 has 1 keyword-triggered primary flag. This is **not** an accurate reflection of the pattern's presence. At CP2 (human review checkpoint), multiple non-keyword co-parenting refusal behaviors were confirmed across the dataset:

- **Simulated cooperation** — surface-level compliance masking actual refusal
- **Medical record exclusion** — Eli excluded from Olivia's medical information and decisions
- **Therapist gatekeeping** — Olivia's therapeutic relationship managed through Melissa as intermediary

The low keyword count reflects the nature of refusal-to-co-parent: it often manifests as absence (not informing, not including) rather than explicit statements that trigger keyword flags.

---

## What This Is

Co-parenting refusal operates through omission and control rather than direct conflict. The required-intermediary system documented in ARC-RF02-002 is itself a form of co-parenting refusal: rather than facilitating direct Eli-Olivia communication and joint parenting decisions, Melissa positioned herself as the exclusive channel for all parenting contact.

---

## Court Argument Value

The medical exclusion and therapist gatekeeping are the most actionable elements here for court filings — they are concrete, verifiable, and directly relevant to parenting plan compliance.
