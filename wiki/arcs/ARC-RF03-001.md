**Status:** ACTIVE

**Date Range:** June 2024 – February 2026

## What This Is

Melissa uses scheduling logistics as a mechanism to deny, complicate, or shift the burden of Eli's court-ordered parenting time. The primary form is **SPP (Eli's court-ordered time) transport offloading** — asserting that Eli is responsible for pickup/dropoff even when the parenting plan requires otherwise, effectively making visits contingent on Eli absorbing additional logistics burden.

5 of 6 signals involve transport offloading. The sixth involves a dance enrollment schedule conflict used to obstruct Eli's time.

---

## Key Signals

**ROW (Jun 2024) — First Transport Offload**
"And once she comes over to your house on weekends it's up to you to bring her home per court order." Logistics framed as Eli's obligation regardless of which party triggered the visit.

**ROW (Nov 2025) — Dance Enrollment Conflict**
Activity enrollment used to create a scheduling conflict with Eli's parenting time — establishing an obligation that competes with his court-ordered access.

## Court Argument Value

Low signal count here does not reflect pattern absence — it reflects that the primary access-denial mechanism is RF-02 (required intermediary, visit refusals). RF-03 represents a secondary mechanism: when direct refusal isn't available, logistics become the obstruction tool.

Cross-documents: RF-02 (access denial), RF-06 (financial — transport costs).
