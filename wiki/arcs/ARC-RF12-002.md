# ARC-RF12-002 — Bait-and-Switch Communication Cycle

**Status:** WATCHING
**RF Category:** [[../patterns/RF-12|RF-12 — Strategic Communication]]
**Signal Count:** 2
**Date Range:** Phase B–C

---

## What This Is

The bait-and-switch cycle documents instances where Melissa establishes a cooperative or positive communication tone, then pivots to hostile, demanding, or manipulative content within the same exchange or shortly after. The "bait" is the cooperative framing; the "switch" is the return to the underlying hostile pattern.

This is related to but distinct from ARC-RF12-001: where RF12-001 documents deliberate strategic framing for the record, RF12-002 documents the cycle of apparent cooperation followed by reversal.

---

## Court Argument Value

Useful for countering any "she's trying to cooperate" narrative: the bait-and-switch pattern shows that surface-level cooperative language is not sustained.

Low signal count reflects the need for Phase 2 synthesis to fully identify and document this pattern across the dataset.
