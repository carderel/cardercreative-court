## Summary

DARVO-WRAPPED FULL-CUSTODY THREAT WITH PRE-CONSTRUCTED COURT NARRATIVE. ADD-002 temporal rule: DARVO-denial-of-threat mechanism = new legal-threat sub-category → Sev-2. Context: Melissa was in the ER; Eli had Olivia and offered to keep her rather than drop her off with Melissa on Percocet. Melissa escalated into a major custody threat cluster. ROW-9957 delivers the explicit custody filing threat weaponizing the ER situation as child-welfare evidence. ROW-9958 is a ~100-word pre-constructed court narrative delivered in real-time — Melissa constructs and delivers the exact argument the judge would receive, deploying inadequate-father framing ('hasn't done in 4 years'), martyrdom (mother 'tries to get better from a medical illness'), and the Percocet context as proof of Eli's prioritization failure. ROW-9960 adds a second explicit threat ('I'll be contacting my lawyer'). ROW-9964 is the defining sub-category marker: immediately after two explicit custody filing threats, Melissa states 'There's no threats' and reframes full custody demand as a non-threatening 'plan of action.' This DARVO-denial-of-threat mechanism is a first in the arc: the act of denying a threat while simultaneously making it adds a gaslighting layer to the legal threat pattern. Functional effect: Eli cannot respond to the threat as a threat without being characterized as misinterpreting a mere 'plan of action'; the denial preemptively delegitimizes his documentation of the threat. Prior full-custody threats (S-016 Aug 2024) lacked this denial mechanism. Combined with the sophisticated court-narrative construction in ROW-9958, this cluster introduces two new qualitative elements to the threat pattern. ADD-001 does not reduce scope: custody filing threats are core RF-05, not Abby-framework attacks.
