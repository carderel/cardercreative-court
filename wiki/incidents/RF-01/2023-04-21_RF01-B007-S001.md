## Summary

This cluster constitutes full activation of the Abby attack vector (RF01-ADD-001) through Olivia's voiced preferences. The sequence: (1) Melissa privately characterizes Abby with extreme hostility to Eli ('that bitch / fucking whore mouth') — this framing almost certainly reflects how Melissa discusses Abby in Olivia's environment; (2) Melissa relays Olivia's 'crying' and demand that Abby not come around (ROW-2379); (3) Six days later, Melissa relays Olivia's 'adamant' refusal to be with Eli if Abby is present (ROW-2399), claiming this is 'her plan all on her own' (ROW-2408). The 'her plan all on her own' claim is a canonical coaching-deflection: when a child has a highly specific, adult-benefit-aligned position, the custodial parent claiming 'she came up with this herself' is a recognized alienation pattern. The result is structural: Olivia's contact with Eli is now conditioned on Abby's exclusion, and Melissa has weaponized Olivia's voiced preferences to achieve this. Since Abby lives with Eli, conditioning Olivia's willingness to be with Eli on Abby's absence creates a direct loyalty conflict between Olivia's attachment to her father and her coached (or at minimum amplified) rejection of Abby. RF01-ADD-002 (Sev 2 minimum): 'Can't handle three hours a week' (ROW-2378) + 'incapable of making good choices' (ROW-2405) constitute sustained inadequate-father framing. RF01-ADD-004: ROW-2379, ROW-2396, ROW-2398, ROW-2399, ROW-2407-2409 all deploy Olivia's voiced reactions as mechanisms for restricting Eli's custodial arrangements.
