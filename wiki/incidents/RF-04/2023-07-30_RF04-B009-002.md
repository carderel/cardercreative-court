## Summary

Incident: Olivia saw what she believed was Eli vaping on a video call. Eli denies he was vaping and offers an alternate explanation. Melissa does not believe him. RF-04 mechanism: Melissa sequences four child-distress relay messages in rapid succession (ROW-3127, 3131, 3135, 3136 — within approximately 3 minutes) designed to maximize the guilt-delivery impact of Olivia's emotional state. Each message escalates: crying → 'cause you upset her' → 'doesn't want to look at you' → 'doesn't want to Google meet you.' This is the ARC-RF04-002 pattern: child's emotional state relayed as guilt instrument rather than communicated once and allowing Eli to address directly with Olivia. ROW-3137 'Don't lie to her. She doesn't deserve that' establishes a pre-conviction frame before the factual dispute is resolved. ROW-3151 (Jul 31) adds an implicit gatekeeping threat: 'I'm the reason she calls you most days. She didn't want to today. She's still mad' — positions Melissa as the facilitator whose cooperation determines whether Eli has a relationship with Olivia; Olivia's anger is presented as a consequence Eli caused. ROW-3158: 'Olivia said you need to give an apology right now and not a fake one either. A real one' — child's demand relayed as authority directive with added qualification ('not a fake one') framing Eli as someone who would give a performative apology unless instructed otherwise.
