# §IV.G — Information Sharing Violations

**SPP Reference:** Section IV.G — Sharing Information
**ORC References:** ORC §3109.051(I) Day Care Access · ORC §3109.051(J) School Activities Access
**Violation Type:** Systematic failure to share school, healthcare, and extracurricular information within 24 hours
**Documentation Type:** Structural / single-channel control (confirmed CP2)

---

## Exact SPP / ORC Language

**§IV.G — Sharing Information:**
> Each parent shall **inform the other parent within 24 hours** of any school event, healthcare event, or extracurricular activity scheduled for or involving the child.
>
> Both parents shall have equal access to all school records, medical records, and other records related to the child.

**ORC §3109.051(I) — Day Care Center Access Notice (Joint SPP page 25-26):**
> THE PARENT WHO IS NOT THE RESIDENTIAL PARENT IS ENTITLED TO ACCESS TO ANY DAY CARE CENTER THAT IS OR WILL BE ATTENDED BY THE CHILD WITH WHOM PARENTING TIME IS GRANTED, TO THE SAME EXTENT THAT THE RESIDENTIAL PARENT IS GRANTED ACCESS TO THE CENTER.

**ORC §3109.051(J) — School Activities Notice (Joint SPP page 26):**
> THE PARENT WHO IS NOT THE RESIDENTIAL PARENT IS ENTITLED TO ACCESS, UNDER THE SAME TERMS AND CONDITIONS AS THE RESIDENTIAL PARENT, TO ANY STUDENT ACTIVITY THAT IS RELATED TO THE CHILD AND TO WHICH THE RESIDENTIAL PARENT OF THE CHILD LEGALLY IS PROVIDED ACCESS. ANY SCHOOL EMPLOYEE OR OFFICIAL WHO KNOWINGLY FAILS TO COMPLY WITH THIS ORDER IS IN CONTEMPT OF COURT.

---

## Documented Status

### Single-Channel Information Control (CP2 Confirmed)

All information about Olivia's life — school events, extracurriculars, health — reaches Eli through a single channel: Melissa's voluntary disclosures. CP2 analysis confirmed:

- **No proactive 24-hour notifications** from Melissa to Eli about school events or appointments
- **All information is Melissa-controlled**: Eli learns about Olivia's activities only when Melissa chooses to share
- **Dance enrollment**: Melissa created the appearance of consulting Eli on Olivia's dance enrollment while the decision was already made; the enrollment conflicted with Eli's parenting time; Eli's input was not incorporated (RF-10, ADD-003)

### School Records Access

ORC §3109.051(J) gives Eli the same right to school records and activities as Melissa. This is a self-executing court order — any school official who denies Eli access to Olivia's records, events, or activities is themselves in contempt.

Whether Eli has been included in school communications (parent portal access, event notifications, teacher conferences) requires direct inquiry — this is not captured in the message dataset.

### Extracurricular Conflicts

The dance enrollment incident (Aug 2025) is the documented case where Melissa enrolled Olivia in an activity conflicting with Eli's parenting time without genuine prior coordination. This is an RF-10 (co-parenting refusal) signal that also violates the §IV.G requirement to share information and involve both parents in scheduling decisions.

---

## The Structural Problem

The §IV.G violation is not a collection of discrete incidents — it is a system. Because all Olivia information flows through Melissa:

1. Eli receives information at Melissa's discretion, not on a 24-hour mandate
2. Information is used strategically — shared when it benefits Melissa, withheld when it doesn't
3. Eli cannot independently verify what he is and isn't being told
4. The required-intermediary system (ARC-RF02-002) is the enforcement mechanism for information control

The ARC-RF02-002 required-intermediary arc and the §IV.G information-sharing violation are two descriptions of the same behavior.

---

## Discovery Targets

To document the §IV.G violation fully:
1. **School parent portal** — does Eli have independent access, or is he reliant on Melissa?
2. **School communications** — are both parents on the distribution list for all school events?
3. **Extracurricular provider records** — who is listed as the primary contact and notification recipient?
4. **Medical provider communications** — are both parents receiving appointment summaries?

---

## Argument Structure

1. The SPP imposes a proactive 24-hour duty on Melissa — not a right to request information, an affirmative obligation to share it
2. The CP2-confirmed single-channel control documents the structural absence of that sharing
3. The dance enrollment incident documents a specific case where information was withheld and Eli's parenting time was affected
4. ORC §3109.051(J) school access is a separate, independent order — any school that has excluded Eli is also in contempt
