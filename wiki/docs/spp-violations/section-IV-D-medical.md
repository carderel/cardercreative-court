# §IV.D — Medical / Health Care Violations

**SPP Reference:** Section IV.D — Health Care
**ORC Reference:** ORC §3109.051(H) — Records Access Notice
**Violation Type:** Exclusion from medical appointments; denial of access to medical records
**Documentation Type:** Structural (confirmed CP2; not keyword-detectable in messages)

---

## Exact SPP Language

**§IV.D — Health Care:**
> Each parent shall **notify the other parent within 24 hours** of any medical appointment, illness, or health-related event concerning the child.
>
> Both parents have the **right to attend all medical, dental, and mental health appointments** for the child.

**ORC §3109.051(H) — Records Access Notice (incorporated in Joint SPP, page 24-25):**
> THE PARENT WHO IS NOT THE RESIDENTIAL PARENT IS **ENTITLED TO ACCESS TO ANY RECORD THAT IS RELATED TO THE CHILD**, UNDER THE SAME TERMS AND CONDITIONS AS THE RESIDENTIAL PARENT, AND TO WHICH SAID RESIDENTIAL PARENT IS LEGALLY PROVIDED ACCESS. **ANY KEEPER OF A RECORD WHO KNOWINGLY FAILS TO COMPLY WITH THIS ORDER IS IN CONTEMPT OF COURT.**

Two separate obligations: (1) Melissa must notify Eli within 24 hours of medical events; (2) Eli has a legal right to access all of Olivia's medical records, and any keeper who denies that access is in contempt.

---

## Documented Status

### Medical Record Access: Zero

**CP2 Confirmed (ADD-007):** Eli has **zero access** to Olivia's medical records. This is structural — not a contested case or a specific incident. Eli has never been informed of Olivia's medical history, current providers, or treatment through any channel other than Melissa's selective disclosures.

This is confirmed through CP2 behavioral analysis, not keyword detection. The evidence type is what is *absent* from the messages: there are no messages from Melissa notifying Eli of upcoming appointments, there are no messages from Eli confirming receipt of such notifications, and Eli has never referenced attending a medical appointment.

### Therapist Gatekeeping

**CP2 Confirmed:** Melissa controls Olivia's therapeutic relationships. She has dropped therapists who challenge her narrative. Eli has no documented access to Olivia's therapist or therapeutic records.

This is both a §IV.D violation (right to attend mental health appointments) and a co-parenting refusal (RF-10).

### Single-Channel Information Control

All medical information about Olivia reaches Eli through a single channel: Melissa's voluntary disclosures. The SPP requires proactive 24-hour notification; the actual practice is no notification unless Melissa chooses to share.

---

## Why This Requires Discovery

Unlike the visit-refusal and communication violations — which are fully documented in text messages — medical exclusion evidence requires:

1. **Subpoena of Olivia's medical records** from her providers (Eli's ORC §3109.051(H) right makes this straightforward)
2. **Court-ordered inquiry** about what Eli has been told regarding Olivia's health
3. **Provider depositions** if providers denied Eli access based on Melissa's instruction

The Records Access Notice in the Joint SPP is self-executing: any medical provider who denies Eli access to Olivia's records, knowing he is a parent on a court order, is themselves in contempt.

---

## Argument Structure

1. The SPP requires 24-hour notification of all medical events — four years of messages document no such notifications
2. The Records Access Notice gives Eli legal right to all of Olivia's medical records
3. Eli's documented exclusion from all medical information is a structural contempt pattern, not individual incidents
4. The therapist-gatekeeping behavior (dropping therapists who challenge Melissa's narrative) is the highest-stakes subset of this pattern — it weaponizes Olivia's mental health treatment against Eli
