# §III.A — Parenting Time Schedule Violations

**SPP Reference:** Section III.A — Parenting Time Schedule
**Violation Type:** Unreasonable refusal of scheduled parenting time
**Documented Violations:** 104 primary RF-02 signals

---

## Exact SPP Language

> Father shall have parenting time every other weekend, beginning Friday at 7:30 p.m. and ending Sunday at 6:00 p.m.
>
> Mother **shall not unreasonably refuse parenting time** to Father.

The operative clause is absolute: "shall not unreasonably refuse." There is no exception for child preference relayed through the other parent.

---

## Why Child Preference Is Not a Defense

Under Ohio law, a minor child's expressed preference does not override a court-ordered parenting schedule — particularly when:

1. The preference is relayed through the custodial parent (not independently expressed to the court)
2. The relaying parent has admitted scripting those conversations (ROW-6819, Aug 21, 2024: *"I've told her to have conversations and I also give her things to tell you about. I remind her of some of the things she's told me."*)
3. Every documented refusal uses the same mechanism: Melissa relays "she doesn't want to go" — there is no independent Olivia-to-Eli communication

Every "she doesn't want to go" relay after ROW-6819 must be read through the coaching admission.

## Ohio Contempt Standard

Ohio courts hold that willful failure to comply with a parenting time order constitutes contempt. Documented pattern evidence across four years — with written text messages — is among the strongest available proof of willfulness.
