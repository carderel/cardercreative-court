# §IV.B — Relocation Notice Violations

**SPP Reference:** Section IV.B — Relocation
**ORC Reference:** Ohio Revised Code §3109.051(G)
**Violation Type:** Failure to file Notice of Intent to Relocate before active house-searching
**Documented Signals:** 4 (ARC-RF02-006)

---

## Exact SPP / ORC Language

**SPP §IV.B:**
> The residential parent shall provide **written notice to the Court and the other parent at least 60 days** prior to relocating the child's residence to a location other than that specified in this Plan.

**ORC §3109.051(G) (incorporated in Joint SPP, page 24-25):**
> IF THE RESIDENTIAL PARENT INTENDS TO MOVE TO A RESIDENCE OTHER THAN THE RESIDENCE SPECIFIED IN THE PARENTING TIME ORDER OR DECREE OF THE COURT, THE RESIDENTIAL PARENT SHALL FILE A NOTICE OF INTENT TO RELOCATE WITH THIS COURT, ADDRESSED TO THE ATTENTION OF THE RELOCATION OFFICER.

Two overlapping requirements: SPP requires 60-day written notice; ORC §3109.051(G) requires a formal court filing with the Relocation Officer.

## The Legal Problem

No Notice of Intent to Relocate is documented anywhere in the dataset. If Melissa was actively searching for houses in August 2025 with intent to relocate out of state:

1. The 60-day written notice to Eli was required at the point active searching began
2. A formal court filing with the Relocation Officer was required
3. Neither appears in the dataset

The Franklin County Auditor record (retrieved March 29, 2026) documents Melissa's current property address and can serve as a baseline for any future relocation argument.

---

## Why This Matters to the Court

Out-of-state relocation would:
- Eliminate Father's every-other-weekend access entirely (geography)
- Convert the required-intermediary system from structural to geographic
- Require judicial modification of all parenting time provisions
- Give Melissa unilateral control over Eli-Olivia contact at the ultimate level

The relocation intent co-occurring with a full-custody threat (Aug 14, 2025) documents that these are connected strategies, not independent thoughts.
