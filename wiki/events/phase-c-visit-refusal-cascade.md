## Overview

Beginning August 26, 2025, Melissa deployed the required-intermediary mechanism (ARC-RF02-002) as a vehicle for **consecutive weekend visit refusals**. This cascade ran through April 2026 and is the central access-denial sequence in the dataset.

The cascade is not a series of isolated events — it is a **sustained access-denial period** enabled by the Melissa-as-required-intermediary infrastructure built across the preceding three years.

## The Broker-Gating Model

By December 2025, Melissa had articulated an explicit broker-gating model:
- Melissa relays Olivia's objections to visiting Eli
- Melissa disclaims responsibility ("not my fault")
- Melissa conditions visit restoration on Eli remedying the relayed complaints
- Melissa positions herself as the evaluator of when Olivia is "ready"

This is the required-intermediary architecture at its most explicit: Eli cannot restore access without going through Melissa, who holds the evaluation criteria.

---

## Why the Cascade Ended (Apr 9, 2026)

Olivia attended Eli's April 9 weekend — but the arc ends "with intermediary structure intact." The restoration did not resolve the intermediary architecture; it demonstrated that when Melissa mediates brokering in Eli's favor, access is restored. This confirms: access is contingent on Melissa's facilitation, not Olivia's genuine preference.

---

## Why This Cascade Matters

The visit-refusal cascade — six consecutive months with no court-ordered resolution — is the most concentrated access-denial period in the dataset and the primary basis for any motion to compel compliance or contempt filing. It is fully documented in text messages.
