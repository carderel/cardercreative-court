# Attorney Wiki — Communication Analysis

## What This Is
A knowledge base built from a structured analysis of ~15,465 text messages between Eli Carder (father) and Melissa (mother) spanning August 2022 through April 2026, in the context of an active family law custody dispute.

This wiki was generated by a multi-pass analysis pipeline that processed every message against 15 behavioral red flag categories. It is an attorney strategy tool — not a court exhibit.

## Parties
- **Eli Carder** — father, plaintiff. Filed divorce July 2021.
- **Melissa** — mother, defendant.
- **Olivia** — child subject of custody evaluation, born approx. 2016-2017.
- **Abby** — Eli's girlfriend; Olivia's cousin through Melissa's family (Abby's mother is Kristi Moose, Melissa's sister). No blood relation to Eli. Relationship began Spring 2020. Together since mid-2020. Melissa repeatedly uses this relationship as an attack vector, falsely characterizing it as incestuous.
- **Alice** — Eli and Abby's biological daughter, born September 2024. Lives with Eli full-time. Olivia's half-sister.
- **Milo** — Abby's son from prior relationship, born ~2019, age 7. Lives with Eli and Abby full-time.
- **Gary** — Melissa's boyfriend, documented overnight presence from August 2025.

## How to Navigate This Wiki

### Start Here
1. Read `wiki/index.md` — master map of all pages and their relationships
2. For a behavioral overview: `wiki/analysis/executive-summary.md`
3. For a specific pattern: navigate to `wiki/patterns/`
4. For a specific person or entity: `wiki/entities/`
5. For arc trajectories (how patterns evolved over time): `wiki/arcs/`
6. For key compound events: `wiki/events/`
7. For chronological view: `wiki/timeline/`

### Search Strategy
- Follow `[[wiki-links]]` between pages to trace relationships
- The index has a full tag listing — use tags to find related pages quickly
- Each pattern page links to its key evidence events
- Each event page links back to the patterns it evidences

### Answering Questions
When asked about a behavioral pattern, person, or event:
1. Read `wiki/index.md` first to orient
2. Navigate to the relevant pattern or entity page
3. Follow links to supporting evidence events
4. Cross-reference arc pages for trajectory context

### Do Not
- Do not read all files before answering — use the index to find what's relevant
- Do not treat individual signals as isolated — always check the arc page for pattern context
- Do not present this analysis as court testimony — it is attorney strategy context

## Analysis Framework Reference

- RF-01: Parental Alienation
- RF-02: Gatekeeping / Access Denial
- RF-03: Weaponized Logistics
- RF-04: Emotional Manipulation (DARVO)
- RF-05: Intimidation / Legal Threats
- RF-06: Financial Coercion
- RF-07: Boundary Violations
- RF-08: Triangulation / Child Coaching
- RF-09: Gaslighting
- RF-10: Refusal to Co-Parent
- RF-11: Inappropriate Child Involvement
- RF-12: Strategic Communication
- RF-13: Escalation Patterns
- RF-14: Substance / Safety
- RF-15: False Allegations / Projection

**Total primary flags: ~364 across 15 passes.**
**Direction: overwhelmingly received (Melissa-to-Eli).**
